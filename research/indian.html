Background

I like my food hot and spicy. It bothers me when I ask for it HOT and SPICY but the restaurant doesn't give it to me like that (for whatever reason). I think Indian restaurants are especially guilty of this, and I suspect it's because of the stereotype that white people can't handle spicy foods. So what better way to test this than an actual experiment?

Methods

Food

I will order some type of meat vindaloo dish because it so well-known as a spicy dish.

Names

I will use the name Steve Johnson for my American identity and Vikas Patil for my Indian identity. Vikas Patil was confirmed to be a very Indian name by my very Indian friend, so the restaurants should be none the wiser.

Ordering

I will order food online 30 minutes between each other. I will randomize which person, Steve or Vikas, goes first. I am not ordering at the same time because the restaurant may prepare both dishes together, ruining any possible difference, while ordering 30 minutes apart minimizes that risk but also increases the likelihood that the staff member preparing the dish is the same person.

No special instructions will be included in the order.
