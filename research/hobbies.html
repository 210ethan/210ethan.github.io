Definition

First, the term "hobby" must be generally defined, followed by examples of what a hobby is and isn't.

  • Merriam-Webster:
    a pursuit outside one's regular occupation engaged in especially for relaxation
  • Dictionary.com:
    an activity or interest pursued for pleasure or relaxation and not as a main occupation
  • Wikipedia:
    A hobby is considered to be a regular activity that is done for enjoyment, typically during one's leisure time.

These are all okay and similar, but not perfect, at least how I would choose to define hobby. For example, hanging out with friends is an activity I enjoy that's outside of my job, but is that really a hobby? Dictionary.com's definition is probably the closest to my own, so I'll plagiarize and simplify to:

an activity pursued for pleasure

This definition gives two important characteristics of a hobby:

  • Goals: There is some type of goal associated with the hobby (indicated by the use of "pursued"), whether improvement, an end product, or competition
  • Enjoyment: The hobbyist derives some amount of satisfaction out of the hobby (indicated by the use of "pleasure"), whether it be from the process or result

Some examples should be given, where the "yes" category is taken from Wikipedia's list of hobbies and the "no" category taken from my mind. There is some overlap between the two, which depends on the hobbyist's purpose.

  • 3-D printing: The hobbyist improves at designing products while also getting a final product that may be useful to them
  • Candle making: The hobbyist improves the quality of their candle and gets a candle out of it
  • Language learning: The hobbyist improves their language skill and can use it in real world situations
  • Mountain biking: The hobbyist improves their bike handling ability and fitness
