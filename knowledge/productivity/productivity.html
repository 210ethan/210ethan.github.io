Productivity: Technology and Practices

Productivity boils down to the optimization and efficiency of time. The systems, applications, and methods used should attempt to minimize both time and effort spent doing monotonous or tedious tasks. Some general principles:

  • Every app used should sync to both desktop and mobile devices. This prevents items like notes or appointments from being inaccessible on either device.
  • The system/method used should consume as little time as possible.
  • The system/method should take as minimal effort as possible.

Time: Organization, Reduction, Optimization

Calendar

First, time needs to be organized. This means using a calendar app that can sync to both desktop and mobile devices: Google Calendar (GC, website, Wikipedia), Microsoft Outlook (website, Wikipedia), and Apple's Calendar (website, Wikipedia) are all well-known, popular choices.

GC will be used as an example.

Separate color-coded calendars can be used for each aspect of your life, e.g., social activities, course work, chores. This is helpful for a few reasons:

  1. The colors allow you to visually see where you are spending a majority of your time.
  2. You can better distinguish between events.
  3. Each individual calendar can be customized with notifications, sharing, and so on.

Everything that needs to be done at a certain time or lasts a certain amount of time gets put on the calendar to remind, help expect what's upcoming, and prevent double-booking.

Time Tracking

Knowing where your time is going serves to remind you exactly just how much time is spent doing something. I can personally get lost watching YouTube videos, only to look up at the clock and see it's 2 hours later than I thought it was.

RescueTime is my go-to software. It automatically tracks apps and websites and sends a weekly report (see below) with a detailed time breakdown.

While RescueTime is good for overall time tracking, only the premium version can give you real-time updates as to how much time you are spending on unproductive sites. That's where apps like StayFocusd come in.

StayFocusd allows for daily time tracking on specific sites that you set. Say YouTube, Reddit, and Twitch are the three websites you spend the most unproductive time on. With StayFocusd, you can limit the aggregate time spent on these three sites. This allows for daily tracking, e.g., if the maximum time allowed is 2 hours and it runs out, you know you've spent 2 hours on unproductive sites for that day. After time runs out, it blocks those sites for the remainder of the active day (unproductive time only contributes to the timer during "Active Hours" on "Active Days").

In the case of completely blocking a website, see the Chrome extension Block Site.

To-Do List

A to-do list (TDL) serves to remind and plan the following day, week, or even month. Any note-taking or word processor will do: Microsoft Word (website, Wikipedia), Microsoft OneNote (website, Wikipedia), and Evernote (website, Wikipedia) are all well-known, popular choices.

Below is what a typical TDL day looks like for for me:

My TDL for a standard day is organized into six sections, each with different subfields:

  • Education: The number fields are for the courses I was taking, the AF field is my work, and the miscellaneous field is for anything that doesn't fit into those categories.
  • Training: Exercise for the day.
  • Daily: Daily habits and reminders for the day.
  • Social/Other: Social activities with friends or family, chores, other miscellaneous tasks.
  • Summary/Thoughts: A place to write down what I did that day or anything I'm feeling.
  • Training (box): Specific details of my training session that day. Helps to track progress over months and years.

Reduction

The best way to reduce computer task time is through a search app: Alfred (website, Wikipedia) and Quicksilver (website, Wikipedia) are popular applications for MacOS. (Not sure about Windows or Linux apps.)

These expedite many processes:

  • Internet search: Instead of opening up a browser, opening up a new tab, going to a specific website, then typing in the search, a simple command button + spacebar brings up the Alfred search bar, followed by a command to search specific sites, e.g., "w alfred" pulls up Alfred's Wikipedia page and "gr war and peace" searches Goodreads for War and Peace. Commands are customizable.
  • File search: Instead of opening Finder and going through many folders to find the file, a simple "Find + [search element name]" in the Alfred search bar finds it immediately. You can also search within files for strings
  • Other: Perform math, access the dictionary, and control MacOS.

Health

Productivity has been found to directly correlate with a variety of health metrics.

Sleep

Getting high-quality sleep is extremely important for improving productivity. In their paper, The Cost of Poor Sleep: Workplace Productivity Loss and Associated Costs, researchers found that "Mean productivity loss (Fig. 2) was significantly higher for the insomnia group (6.1%) than for the at-risk (4.6%) and good-sleep (2.5%) groups (all P  0.05). The ISS group had an intermediate level of productivity loss (5.5%)."
