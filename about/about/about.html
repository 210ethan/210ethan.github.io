Personality

My Big Five personality traits scores (percentiles) using Open Psychometrics and Truity. Actual scores from each test are separated by the slash.

  • Extroversion: 63/67. I am very comfortable around people I know or have something to talk about with, but do not enjoy big parties where I don't know very many people.
  • Neuroticism: 57/29. I am known as a very happy and positive person amongst my friend group. I can get caught up on negative thinking loops, but it's rare.
  • Agreeableness: 67/60. I am known as a very kind and empathetic person amongst my friend group. I sympathize with others' problems and emotions easily.
  • Conscientiousness: 93/96. I am very exacting in a task, do not like procrastination, and enjoy working.
  • Openness to experience: 40/69. I see myself as very intellectually curious (see my Links page for the variety of subjects I read about), but am not incredibly open to new experiences, especially when the outcome is unknown or random. I do not view myself as very creative.

Writing Style

I am making it a point to only write when I want to. No forcing myself, no saying just 15 minutes, no guilting. Myself and others have found that hobbies' fun factors tend to quickly fade once they become a "chore".

I write about things that interest me (although there may be some parts within the subject that don't interest me).

Website

I try to keep the website design as simple as possible:

  • All public pages are accessible from the home page.
  • Hyperlinks are used instead of footnotes, endnotes, or sidenotes (I use parentheses like this in lieu of those types of notes).
  • Default HTML font and sizes are used.
  • CSS is simple.

Other notes:

  • I block people's faces with a black square in personal pictures and only mention initials/vague names for anonymity.
  • I do not use any analytics software.
  • The website is hosted using GitHub Pages.
  • I link all books to Goodreads. The reviews are generally more reliable and there are multiple purchase options other than Amazon (although I highly recommend BookFinder).
