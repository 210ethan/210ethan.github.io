Home Gym: Setup and Equipment

Why you should get a home gym, how to set it up, and equipment suggestions. (Some of this text is copied from the Home Gym section of my Life Improvements post.)

Motivation

A personal home gym poses a few distinct advantages compared to the stereotypical commercial gym.

Cost

Cost of a home gym depends heavily on a few things:

  • Quality and brand of equipment: Rogue Fitness equipment will cost a lot more than some Walmart-brand squat rack or barbell, but quality is vastly superior.
  • Breadth of equipment: Do you want a rowing machine? What about a SkiErg? A few kettlebells? Grippers? While these are not truly necessary for "fitness", they are helpful, albeit expensive.
  • Used vs. new: Craigslist and Facebook Marketplace can offer great deals on used equipment, but may sacrifice quality.

Overall, my total amount spent on home gym equipment is as follows:

  • Powerlifting: (Squat rack, adjustable bench, 45 lb barbell, 255 lb of weights (2x45, 2x35, 2x25, 2x10, 4x5, 2x2.5)), 2x45 plates, belt

  • Dumbbells: 2x20, 2x15, 2x12, 2x10, 2x8, 2x5, 2x3 lb
  • Calisthenics: Dip/pull-up tower, dip belt, gymnastics rings, ceiling mount
  • Grip: Pinch block, thick grips, grippers (x2)
  • Conditioning: Concept2 rower, Schwinn Air-Dyne Ergometer

The average commercial gym costs "between $40 and $50 a month" = $480-600 per year = $540 average. The lowest price I've seen is Planet Fitness at $10/month = $120/year. Depending on the membership cost, the breakeven point is:

\[\text{breakeven_avg} = \frac{$2320}{$540\text{/yr}} = 4.30 \, \text{yr}\] \[\text{breakeven_cheap} = \frac{$2320}{$120\text{/yr}} = 19.33 \, \text{yr}\]

Convenience

No more driving to the gym or making sure to go during business hours. Simply go to the home gym in 10 seconds at any time. Driving and time saved:

\[\text{miles_saved} = (2-4) \, \text{mi/drive} \times (3-5) \, \text{drive/wk} \times 50 \, \text{wk/yr} = (300-2000) \, \text{mi/yr}\] \[\text{gas_money_saved} = (300-2000) \, \text{mi/yr} \times \frac{1}{20} \, \text{gal/mi} \times $2.50/\text{gal} = $(40 - 250)/\text{yr}\] \[\text{time_saved} = (10-30) \, \text{min/drive} \times (3-5) \, \text{drive/wk} \times 50 \, \text{wk/yr} = (25-125) \, \text{hr/yr}\]

Style

No judgment, no one to tell you you're being too loud or annoying (except maybe the police and neighbors), wear (or not wear) whatever you want.

Equipment
