Mind-Numbing Training

Training until the mind (and sometimes other body parts) are numb with boredom or pain offers both benefits and detriments in one's training.

Contents

  • Definition
  • See Also

Definition

Mind-numbing training consists of challenging, repetitive, or boring tasks, or a combination thereof, that are of a long duration.

Examples of mind-numbing sessions include:

  • Climbing the same hill for hours in preparation for Everesting
  • Doing 3000 burpees in a day
  • Smolov sets
  • Sitting on a trainer for hours with little to no distractions (music, book, television)
  • Single, long set of one exercise (1, 2)

Non-examples include:

  • Few hard, short repeats on [airbike/rower/SkiErg]
