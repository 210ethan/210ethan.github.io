Wrist and Ankle Strengthening

A few notes before beginning:

  • Before strength is gained in any position, the position's mobility must be developed.
  • There is such thing as perfect alignment, but it's uncommon and often the best-case scenario. Train for worst-case scenario (poor alignment), because it WILL happen.
  • Why train wrists and ankles? Both are the required blocks to basic human movement. Walking, running, and standing require the ankles. Activities requiring basic dexterity use the wrists, among other common tasks (typing, writing).

Wrists

There are a few different types of wrist movement: flexion, extension, supination, pronation, ulnar deviation, and radial deviation. (Supination and pronation are arguably independent of the wrist.)
