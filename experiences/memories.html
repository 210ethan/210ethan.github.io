Purpose

I want to make my life feel as long as possible. Who wants to feel like their life is short?

There are a few ways to do this:

  1. Make time feel slower (see Slowing Time)
  2. Be able to look back on a time period and recount many memories. This makes the time period feel more full and relates directly to the typical "do novel things" advice given when asked how to slow down time.

I already practice both: I regularly follow the suggestions I laid out, which includes presence, novelty, and documentation in the form of pictures and journaling. But what if I took journaling one step further and tried to document every single memory that comes to my mind? I would have an extremely long list, and being able to look back on each unique memory would surely make my life feel incredibly full.

List of Memories

The list is subdivided into portions of my life that make sense to me. Some memories may be repeated. Identifying information is initialized, changed, or altogether redacted. I purposely lack capitalization, punctuation, and grammar to more quickly write.

I predict I'll have over 5,000 memories up to the point of me starting this (19 November 2023).
