AggieFab Reflections

First, a brief history and description of my role at AggieFab.

AggieFab is a shared-user nanofabrication facility open to anyone who pays the membership and equipment fees. Texas A&M students, other university's students, and private companies are welcome to join and use the facilities. The lab has tools that allow users to create devices on the micro- (one-millionth of one meter, or 10-6 m) and nano-scale (one billionth of one meter, or 10-9 m). Users vary heavily on discipline—while AggieFab is under the electrical engineering department's umbrella, students from materials science, chemistry, petroleum engineering, and even pharmaceuticals sciences. The projects are equally as diverse: from microfluidics to GaN-based technologies to waveguides, there is a bit of everything (as long as it needs to be physically fabricated).

I began working there as a student technician in October 2017 and ended in May 2020 — 2.5 years total, excluding the summer of 2019, when I was interning for Texas Instruments. I worked 20 hours per week every week, except for shorter weeks or holidays (where I worked anywhere between 20-39 hours).

I had three primary responsibilities:

  1. Training and advising of new users. Whenever new or existing users needed training on tools, I helped them to learn the tool's operation and theory and answered any questions they had that was unique to their project.
