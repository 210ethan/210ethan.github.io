Causes on an Activist's Radar

ChatGPT 4o came up with the following list after being prompted with "name a bunch of causes a social justice warrior cares about":

  • Racial Equality: Advocating against racism and promoting equality for all races.
  • Gender Equality: Fighting for women's rights and gender nonconformity, including transgender and non-binary issues.
  • LGBTQ+ Rights: Supporting the rights of lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and queer individuals.
  • Economic Justice: Addressing income inequality, advocating for fair wages, and fighting against poverty.
  • Environmental Justice: Working towards sustainable practices and policies that protect the environment and ensure communities are not disproportionately affected by environmental hazards.
  • Disability Rights: Ensuring accessibility and equal opportunities for people with disabilities.
  • Immigrant Rights: Supporting fair treatment and opportunities for immigrants and refugees.
  • Criminal Justice Reform: Addressing issues like police brutality, mass incarceration, and the death penalty.
  • Reproductive Rights: Advocating for women's right to choose and access to reproductive healthcare.
  • Healthcare Access: Ensuring that all individuals have access to affordable and quality healthcare.
  • Educational Equity: Promoting equal access to education and fighting against systemic biases in educational institutions.
  • Indigenous Rights: Supporting the rights and sovereignty of Indigenous peoples.
  • Workers' Rights: Advocating for the rights of workers, including safe working conditions and the right to unionize.
  • Anti-Violence: Addressing issues of domestic violence, sexual assault, and other forms of interpersonal violence.
  • Age Equality: Fighting against ageism and advocating for the rights of the elderly.

And yet, lookism, the "prejudice or discrimination toward people who are considered to be physically unattractive" (which can also be formed as pretty privilege), is not mentioned. Surely it qualifies to be included given the common themes of diversity, inclusion, equality, and fairness? Life is definitely better for the attractive than the unattractive, ceteris paribus. Judging someone based on their race, age, height, gender, disability, or intelligence is frowned upon, so why isn't looks? Or if it is frowned upon, why isn't anyone doing anything about it?

The (Unattractive) Elephants in the Room

Lookism and pretty privilege is arguably well-known, just not talked about for a few potential reasons.

Moloch

Moloch returns, but this time he's hot af. Liv Boeree summarizes the relationship between Moloch and lookism well in her Moloch & Beauty Wars:

If Attractive Alice chooses to forgo makeup and nice clothes on her path towards improving the universal treatment of Ugly Olgas, she will also forgo a number of benefits the makeup provides: better pay (1, 2, 3), free stuff (anecdotes galore here), better treatment from both men and women, the list goes on.

But her compet...friends, the ones who keep wearing makeup and dressing well, will continue reaping the benefits of better raises, free dinners and drinks, etc. Why should Alice suffer while these women prosper? That's not fair! And thus the endless cycle of competition begins.

Recognition of Varying Degrees of Beauty

It's naive, ignorant, but comfortable to claim everyone's equally beautiful, at least in their own way. Beauty is subjective culture-by-culture, meaning it's effectively objective culture-by-culture: an attractive American may not be attractive in a countries practicing leblouh, but she's likely to be attractive across all of America.
