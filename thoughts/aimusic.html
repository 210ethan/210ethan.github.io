Motivation

Mnemonic devices are considered effective learning tools from countless studies:

  • RadoviÄ‡ & Manzey, 2019
  • Wolgemuth, Cobb, & Alwell, 2008
  • Scruggs and Mastropieri, 2000
  • Yalch, 1991
  • Murakami, 2017

Music is a prominent device; remembering song lyrics, even longer information like phone numbers (see (or hear in your head) the song 867-5309/Jenny), is significantly easier than remembering similar series of words without the singer's voice and instruments in the background. Coupling this with the fact that music is fun to listen to and AI-generated musical abilities are improving rapidly and the following idea comes naturally.

Implementation

Lyrics

Lyrics can be generated using a large-language model (LLM) using fill-in-the-blank prompt like:

Create a(n) [number]-verse [style of music, e.g., country, rap, metal] that teaches the listener [information]

Extra conditions or qualifiers can be added, such as:

  • Have a [music style] feature in the middle
    • Ex: rap in the middle of a country song
  • In the style of [artist]
    • Ex: Drake, Lil Yachty, Tim McGraw
  • Use keywords such as [keyword1], [keyword2]
    • Ex: if talking about transistors, keywords can be Fermi level and conduction band
  • Use a level of detail a [grade level] grader could understand
    • Ex: third grader, if the song is targeted at a third-grade audience
  • Have a [type of mood] mood
    • Ex: upbeat, gloomy, melancholy

It's important that the song is catchier and more entertaining than it is informative, else the listener will be turned off and not listen, defeating the entire purpose. Disguising the information within the lyrics is the best option, but requires superb LLM prompting skills.

Generation

There are plenty of AI music generators available on the internet. I used Suno AI for the examples below.

Combination with Current Learning Methods

Music cannot replace the tried-and-true practice of good ol' studying and practice problems, but it can supplement it. Listening to the music regularly and attempting to sing along will help commit it to memory.
