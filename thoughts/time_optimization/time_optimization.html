Time Optimization

A majority of human life seems to resolve around maintenance. We spend an hour or two grocery shopping every week, an hour every day cooking and eating, countless hours commuting (conservative average is approximately 208 hours per year), and so on and so forth. Some of these activities are enjoyable: cooking and savoring a delicious filet mignon brings great satisfaction to some, while others would prefer to make a quick microwave dinner so they can get on with their lives. But why not try to optimize the time spent without sacrificing any of the pleasure?

This essay will examine (mostly through examples) multiple ways to optimize the time spent doing both maintenance and enjoyable activities. It is not meant to be an obsessive way to fill the day spent at 100% intensity and focus, but rather a way to make the unproductive times more productive and enjoyable. If you enjoy commuting in and of itself, don't let this convince you to change your ways. If you absolutely loath the necessary evil of commuting, then the ideas here may be for you.

Automation

Computers exist to do our bidding, making our lives exponentially easier as a result. One of the most impactful aspects is the level of automation computers and software provide. Events that are recurring, triggered, or one-time can be performed by the computer, taking the burden of remembering and performing off of the user.

So take a moment to think: are there things on my computer or in my life that I can easily automate? Here are two personal examples:

  • DailyUpdate: Python script that automatically sends an email containing the day's weather, top news stories (from multiple choosable sources), stock prices, and to-do lists (if you posted them on Reddit like the author). A cron job was scheduled to execute this task every morning, so anyone running it would wake up to an email with all the day's information. So, instead of having to manually search for the weather, stock prices, and multiple news outlets, it was all done in less than a second.
  • Book reviews are collected here. In order to create the HTML table, the Goodreads library export file is used. This is a hassle to remember and perform, so another cron job is used on a Python script that automatically downloads the most recent library and updates the table to reflect any changes.

There are hundreds of other examples:

  • A Programmable coffee maker saves you time in the morning by automatically making your coffee at a set time.
  • The Nest Thermostat learns about your tendencies (work time, preferred temperatures, etc) and automatically adjusts the temperature to reflect those. No more adjusting the thermostat before leaving for work or because it's too warm. Time and money saved.
  • Automate the Boring Stuff with Python is a free book to improve mundane tasks. Tired of doing boring, manual spreadsheet-ing? Read the Excel spreadsheets chapter.
  • Make automatic bill payments for credit cards, mortgages, etc.
