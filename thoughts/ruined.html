Myself

[name] has a had a profound impact on me. I don't consider myself a fanboy because I don't obsess about him as a person, but rather his philosphy, which boils down to a few main points:

  • Do what you say you're going to do
  • You are capable of more than you think you are
  • Don't (fucking) quit
  • Continue growing

Some of these have taken longer for me to implement into my life, but they're all there now, regularly being practiced and refined. I hold myself accountable for following through on commitments and promises. I regularly push my comfort level to remind myself of what I'm capable of. I only quit when it doesn't make sense for me to continue. I seek growth in all aspects of my life.

Others

But it appears others rarely follow these principles. They make empty promises. They don't care about where their limits are, much less pushing past them. They quit when things get challenging. They are complacent with where they're at in life, whether it be professionally or athletically or socially.

And I often disdain them for it.

I abhor those who tell me they'll do something and make no obvious effort to actually do it. I pity those who do not search for their limits. I am annoyed by those who quit at the slightest experience of discomfort. I am disgusted by those who don't seek to better themselves in some fashion.

I know this comes across as condescending, elitist, and derogatory. The saving grace of these four principles is they apply to many aspects of life and can be practiced by most, nature or nurture factors aside. I do not ask for a 99th-percentile rating in all of them. I just want honest effort put into at least one or two.
