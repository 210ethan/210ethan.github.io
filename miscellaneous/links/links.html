June 2020

  • The Secrets of the World's Greatest Art Thief: Social engineering at its finest, Stéphane Breitwieser stole more than $1.4 billion worth of art, most of the time in the middle of the afternoon right under people's noses. [!unique, !history]
  • Brocken spectre: A massive shadow produced by standing atop a mountain when the sun is beginning to rise. Mist or low-hanging clouds required. [!nature]
  • Quantum suicide and immortality (basic explanation here): Interesting thought experiment based on the many-worlds interpretation. [!science]
  • Chicago Tylenol murders: A series of murders in Chicago after someone poisoned Tylenol (pre-tamper-resistant packaging) with potassium cyanide. This led to tamper safety seals being implemented across many industries, with Johnson & Johnson leading the response. [!history]
  • ReviewMeta: Checks the reliability of Amazon reviews and filters out unreliable/fake reviews, giving the user and adjusted product rating. How it works here. [!useful]
  • Electrostatic Wall: "This 'invisible wall' was strong enough to prevent humans from passing through". Explanation here. [!unique, !science]
  • Physics Special Topics archive from University of Leicester: A collection of funny, interesting, and bizarre papers from physics students. Titles include "How Many Lies Could Pinocchio Tell?", "How Hot Is Tatooine", and "How Hungry Was The Very Hungry Caterpillar?". [!science, !unique]
  • The unreasonable effectiveness of one-on-ones: A great write-up of how one-on-ones improved the author's SO's life and his management style. This motivated me to start 1:1's. The main topics the author discussed with his employees were: personal habits and self-improvements, project management, communication, alignment, and uncertainties. You can see Guzey's personal writeup here. Mine to be posted here (empty for now). [!communication, !productivity]
  • Networking for Nerds: Essay discussing how to go about networking, especially for so-called "nerds." Tactics are suggested, but comes down to the main point: make it easy. [!communication]
  • How to make friends over the internet: The main part of meeting people is simply reaching out, and ocial media (especially Twitter and Reddit) easily allow that. On social media, "be somebody people want to follow" and "create the opportunities to be discovered" (simply by reaching out). Supplementary Twitter Guide by Lama Al Rajih here. [!communication]
  • Twitching with Twight: One of Mark Twight's most famous essays, basically telling you to screw the recipe life ("mix one high school diploma with an undergrad degree … a poorly built house in a cul de sac … working for a board that doesn't give a shit about you") and live how you want. I first read this in his book, Kiss or Kill. If you enjoy this piece, I suggest reading the book. RAZE Anthology is a good follow-up on Twight's philosophy and outlook on life. [!philosophy]
  • Atomik Vodka: Website for vodka produced from the Chernobyl zone near Pripyat. Distillation was used to reduce radiation from the grain (paper here). [!nature, !unique, !science]
  • Who's downloading pirated papers? Everyone: Everyone, including institutions with journal access, are using Sci-Hub to access papers. Convenience and reliability trumps the institution's lack of library structure and inconsistency in paper results. Started by Kazakhstani student Alexandra Elbakyan, who is now in hiding due to major lawsuit filed by Elsevier. [!science]
  • 4:01 mile training log of Steve Magness, a high-schooler who ran 4:01 for a full mile. Goes to show the time commitment and dedication require. [!fitness]
  • Internet Search Tips: A collection of tips and tricks to make Google searches more effective. Adding a basic term like "site:[website]" can filter many results, allowing the search results to be more concise and desirable. [!productivity]
  • The Man with the Golden Airline Ticket: The story of the American Airlines AAirpass and Steven Rothstein's experience using it. For just $250,000, the cardholder can fly an unlimited amount of miles for as long as they live—no catch. For an extra $150,000, the cardholder can add a travel companion with the same privileges. AA grossly underestimated Rothstein's yearly travel, eventually finding a loophole in the terms and conditions to revoke his pass due to so-called fraudulent behavior (Rothstein would offer strangers to be his designated companion, leaving AA out of one ticket—this added up).
  • The Great Paper Caper: The story of Frank Bourassa, a career criminal who was able to counterfeit $200 MM using an offset printer, a German paper company that supplied 75/25% cotton/linen paper, and a farmer's garage.
